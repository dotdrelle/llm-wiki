# Writing Standards

## Audience

These deliverables are written for a mixed audience of product managers, engineers, and senior leadership. Assume no prior context beyond what is documented in the wiki.

## Tone and style

- Use plain, direct language. Avoid jargon unless it is defined in the wiki.
- Prefer short sentences and active voice.
- Each factual claim must be followed by a citation in the form `[src: wiki/path/to/page.md]`.
- If a claim cannot be sourced from the wiki, write "not yet documented" rather than inferring.

## Structure rules

- Every section must have at least one sentence before any bullet list.
- Decision rationale must be explicit: state *why*, not just *what*.
- Open questions or gaps must be listed in a dedicated "Missing information" section at the end.

## What to avoid

- Do not invent names, dates, figures, or commitments not present in the wiki.
- Do not summarize by paraphrasing the same sentence twice.
- Do not use filler phrases such as "it is important to note that" or "as mentioned above".
